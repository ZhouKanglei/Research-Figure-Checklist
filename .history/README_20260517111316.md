# Research Figure Checklist

A practical guide for creating clear, consistent, and publication-ready research figures.

This repository provides checklists, examples, plotting templates, color palettes, and case studies for improving research figures. Although the examples mainly come from computer science, machine learning, computer vision, and AI papers, many principles are broadly applicable to research figures in different disciplines.

## Why This Repository?

Figures are not just decorations in a paper. A good research figure should help readers quickly understand the main idea, experimental evidence, and key conclusion.

However, many early-stage researchers often face similar problems:

- The figure does not clearly support the paper's main claim.
- The visual style is inconsistent across different figures.
- Colors are too random or not colorblind-friendly.
- Fonts and line widths are too small after resizing.
- The plotting code is hard to reproduce or modify.
- Captions only describe the figure but do not summarize the key finding.

This repository aims to provide a practical checklist and reusable templates to help researchers create better paper figures.
